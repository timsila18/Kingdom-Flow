# Prompt 9 - Giving, Stewardship and Contribution Management

Prompt 9 adds a privacy-conscious giving and stewardship foundation for offerings, tithes where used, missions, welfare, projects, partnerships, pledges, restricted funds, receipts and reconciliation.

Core principles:

- church funds remain church funds under church governance;
- KingdomFlow does not charge a percentage on giving, donations, offerings, pledges, missions or welfare contributions;
- technology fees remain separate and apply only to defined platform services such as paid programmes, paid event registration, optional processing integrations, premium integrations and subscriptions;
- individual giving is visible only to explicitly authorized finance roles;
- restricted funds preserve donor, church, legal, project, programme, branch, time and purpose restrictions;
- verified financial records use correction and reversal workflows rather than silent edits;
- the product never creates top-giver rankings, generosity scores, pressure dashboards or giving-based spiritual status.

Implemented foundations:

- configurable giving categories, funds and fund restrictions;
- church-owned payment destinations for Paybill, Till and bank instructions with approval and verification states;
- M-Pesa/STK integration-ready architecture through destination, reference, idempotency and manual fallback metadata;
- identified, anonymous, service, group, event, programme, pledge, partnership and manual contribution records;
- contribution verification, duplicate reference detection and restricted reclassification controls;
- cash collections, count teams, handover/deposit metadata and separation-of-duties checks;
- receipt numbering, issuance, voiding and public verification with minimal exposed data;
- member portal, donor statements, pledges, partnerships, campaigns, disputes, refunds and reconciliation imports;
- privacy-aware dashboards and reports with individual giving redacted unless explicitly authorized.

Reserved for Prompt 10: accounting journals, ledger balances, procurement, disbursement posting, real bank reconciliation automation, inventory/stock treatment, completed refund payouts and full accounting statements.
